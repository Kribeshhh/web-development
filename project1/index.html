Culture & Heritage

Experience Nepal's rich traditions and spiritual heritage

A Tapestry of Traditions

Nepal is a land of profound spiritual significance and ancient traditions. With a blend of Hindu and Buddhist cultures, Nepal offers visitors a unique opportunity to experience sacred temples, vibrant festivals, and warm hospitality.

From the colorful celebrations of Dashain and Tihar to the spiritual practices at Pashupatinath Temple, every corner of Nepal tells a story of devotion and cultural pride.

Travel Tips

Essential information for your Nepal adventure

Best Time to Visit

October to November and February to March offer the best weather with clear skies and comfortable temperatures.

Currency & Money

The Nepalese Rupee (NPR) is the official currency. ATMs are widely available in major cities. Credit cards are accepted in tourist areas.

Getting There

Tribhuvan International Airport in Kathmandu is the main entry point. Regular flights connect Nepal to major Asian cities.

Accommodation

Nepal offers a range of options from luxury hotels to budget hostels and homestays. Book in advance during peak seasons.

Food & Dining

Try local dishes like momos, dal bhat, and gundruk. Vegetarian options are abundant. Street food is delicious but choose wisely.

Health & Safety

Altitude sickness is common in high regions. Stay hydrated, acclimatize gradually, and carry basic medications. Nepal is generally safe for tourists.
